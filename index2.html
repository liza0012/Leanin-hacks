Women Equality

A woman’s quest for equality with man is a universal phenomenon. What exists for men is demanded by women. Almost 50% of the world’s population is women, so treating them equally gives an impetus to the development of the society as a whole....The man of the house is traditionally considered to be the breadwinner of the family and the woman is considered to be the homemaker. Tradition and culture are considered to be the two big impediments for women empowerment.Women Empowerment is understood as a very narrow term in today’s world. It may be understood as enabling people, especially women to acquire and possess power resources in order to make decisions on their own or resist decisions made by others that affect them. A person may be said to be powerful when she/he has control over a large portion of power resources such as personal wealth, education, information, knowledge, social status, position, leadership, capabilities of mobilisation. The National Policy on Education (1986) suggested certain strategies to empower women. Accordingly, women became empowered through collective reflections and decision making.

Menstrual Hygiene

Due to women's reproductive structure special features, it makes the female reproductive system particularly vulnerable to the virus, especially during menstruation,... leading to the immunity drop of reproductive organs , more fragile than usual, make it very vulnerable to bacterial infections and develop all kinds of disease , therefore, women in the menstrual period must pay attention to the health and scientific method of using sanitary napkins, to ensure that health. Using sanitary napkins needs to pay attention to the points below: 1. The sanitary napkin is best used when you use it . The sanitary napkin is best to be purchased every month, and can be stored in a small cloth bag that can be put in one cotton, which should be kept in a special place when being carried outdoors.2. Internal adjustment can be completely relaxed. Listen to more soothing music during the menstrual period and do some gentle sports or sports. On the diet, eat less high-fat, high-fat foods, eat more tomatoes, watermelon, cucumbers, beans, vegetables and other light foods. In a good mood, reasonable dietary structure can increase the resistance of the body to foreign bacteria and toxins, and reduce transmission and seizure. 3. Wash hands before using the sanitary napkins so as not to contaminate the sanitary napkin with the germs; Sanitary napkins and pads and tampons should be changed in about two hours, even if the amount is small. In addition,Mainly use shower during the period ,avoid to use bath, also should reduce the number of times that wash bath in public place.

#MeToo Movement

is an effort to effect social change, organized primarily through social media, where it’s often expressed as #MeToo. Originally founded in 2006, it became prominent both online and in the mainstream in late 2017, when several high-profile actresses opened up about their experiences with sexual harassment in the film industry....Me Too movement founder: Tarana Burke During the early years, Tarana Burke, an American social activist, began to use the phrase “me too” on the social networking platform Myspace to highlight the occurrence of sexual harassment, particularly as it targeted women of color. Burke argues that the Me Too movement works on empowerment through empathy by showing the world just how common sexual harassment is and by telling survivors that they aren’t alone — and are supported.
